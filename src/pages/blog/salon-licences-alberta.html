1. Municipal business licence (Calgary & Edmonton) 1. Giấy phép kinh doanh thành phố

Every salon in Alberta operates under a municipal business licence. There is no province-wide salon licence — licensing is handled by each city. For a nail, hair, beauty, or barber shop, the category you need is "Personal Service". Both Calgary and Edmonton use this exact label.

City of Calgary — Personal Service business licence

In Calgary, the 2026 Personal Service business licence breaks down like this:

  • Base fee: $172 new / $131 renewal
  • Fire fee: $72 new / $69 renewal
  • Planning fee: $41 new / $0 renewal
  • Total: $285 new / $200 renewal per year

Tattoo, microblading, and permanent makeup fall under a separate sub-category (Personal Service — tattoo) but the total fees are identical. If you operate as an independent chair operator — renting a chair in someone else's salon — Calgary offers a cheaper licence at $172 new / $131 renewal with no fire or planning fees, because the building inspection has already been done by the primary licensee.

Calgary also charges a $785 non-resident business fee if your salon is based outside Calgary but you operate within city limits (for example, mobile nail or beauty services). This is a one-time surcharge on top of your home city's licence.

Apply through Calgary's business licensing portal or in person at the Planning Services Centre (3rd floor, 800 Macleod Trail SE). Fire inspection is scheduled during the application process and takes roughly 5 business days. Expect your licence to be issued 5 to 15 business days after inspection passes.

City of Edmonton — Personal Service (Tier 3)

Edmonton uses a tiered fee structure under Bylaw 20002. Personal Service — which covers salons, barbers, nail techs, estheticians, and massage — is a Tier 3 business category. Edmonton's fees for Tier 3 in the current schedule are:

  • 1-year licence: $265 new / $239 renewal
  • 2-year licence: $509 new / $457 renewal
  • Non-resident surcharge: $479 for a 1-year non-resident licence, $957 for 2-year

Edmonton's 2-year option is worth considering for salons that want to lock in fees and avoid the annual renewal paperwork. On a per-year basis, 2-year works out to about $254/year new — slightly cheaper than paying annually, and $3 less than Calgary's annual fee.

Apply through the City of Edmonton business licence portal. Fire inspection is handled separately by Edmonton Fire Rescue Services; development compliance is reviewed by Planning and Development.

Mỗi salon ở Alberta hoạt động dưới một giấy phép kinh doanh thành phố. Không có giấy phép salon cấp tỉnh — việc cấp phép do từng thành phố xử lý. Cho tiệm nail, tóc, làm đẹp, hoặc barber, danh mục bạn cần là "Personal Service". Cả Calgary và Edmonton đều dùng đúng tên gọi này.

City of Calgary — Giấy phép Personal Service

2. Alberta Health Services Personal Services Notification 2. Thông báo AHS Personal Services

This is the step most new salon owners either miss or misunderstand. Alberta Health Services does not issue a traditional paid "health permit" for personal service businesses. Instead, under the Personal Services Regulation (effective July 1, 2020), every salon must submit a Personal Services Notification to AHS before opening or before adding any new service.

Key facts:

  • Cost: Free. No licence or permit fee.
  • Required for: Hair, nails, aesthetics, facials, tanning, piercing, tattooing, microblading, eyelash extensions, waxing — essentially any service that enhances, preserves, or alters a client's appearance.
  • How to submit: Online through the AHS Environmental Public Health case management portal at ephisahs.albertahealthservices.ca.
  • Before you open: You can also request a voluntary plan review before construction starts — this prevents costly structural changes later.
  • Inspections: Public health inspectors may visit to verify compliance with the Personal Services Standards, which cover cleaning, disinfection, sterilization, handwashing, ventilation, waste handling, and worker training.
  • Questions: Call AHS Environmental Public Health at 1-833-476-4743 or visit ahs.ca/eph.

Even though there is no fee, treat this as a hard requirement. Operating a salon without notifying AHS puts you in violation of the Public Health Act, and an inspector arriving unannounced can order you to stop offering services immediately. The paperwork takes under 30 minutes online.

If you offer any service that punctures skin or mucous membrane — piercing, tattoo, microblading — you also need written client-care instructions, sterilized critical equipment, and sharps containers, and AHS is likely to inspect your facility proactively.

Đây là bước mà hầu hết chủ salon mới hoặc quên hoặc hiểu sai. Alberta Health Services không cấp "giấy phép y tế" trả phí cho doanh nghiệp dịch vụ cá nhân. Thay vào đó, theo Personal Services Regulation (có hiệu lực 1 tháng 7, 2020), mọi salon phải nộp Thông báo Personal Services cho AHS trước khi mở hoặc thêm dịch vụ mới.

  • Chi phí: Miễn phí. Không có phí giấy phép.
  • Áp dụng cho: Tóc, nail, thẩm mỹ, chăm sóc da, nhuộm da, xỏ khuyên, xăm, nối mi, tẩy lông — về cơ bản mọi dịch vụ làm đẹp.
  • Cách nộp: Trực tuyến tại ephisahs.albertahealthservices.ca.
  • Thanh tra: Thanh tra viên có thể đến kiểm tra tuân thủ Personal Services Standards.
  • Hỏi đáp: Gọi AHS Environmental Public Health tại 1-833-476-4743.

Mặc dù miễn phí, hãy coi đây là yêu cầu bắt buộc. Kinh doanh salon mà không thông báo cho AHS là vi phạm Public Health Act.

3. Trade name registration 3. Đăng ký tên thương hiệu

If you plan to operate your salon under any name that isn't your exact legal name — for example, "Luxe Nail Studio" instead of "Thi Nguyen" — you need to register a trade name with Alberta Corporate Registry. If you operate as a corporation, incorporation handles this automatically. But for sole proprietors and partnerships, the trade name registration is a separate mandatory step.

Cost: $60 government fee. Registry agents (authorized private offices that process these for Alberta) typically charge an additional service fee of $40 to $75, so expect total cost of about $100 to $135.

Where: Any Alberta Registry Agent office. Walk-in service, typically completed same day.

Renewal: Every 5 years.

If you skip this step and your city business licence application uses a trade name, your licence will usually be rejected until the trade name is registered. Handle this first — it takes 15 minutes and unlocks everything downstream.

Nếu bạn dự định mở salon dưới tên khác tên pháp lý của mình — ví dụ "Luxe Nail Studio" thay vì "Thi Nguyen" — bạn phải đăng ký tên thương hiệu với Alberta Corporate Registry.

Chi phí: Phí chính phủ $60. Đại lý đăng ký thường tính thêm $40-$75 phí dịch vụ, tổng khoảng $100-$135.

Gia hạn: 5 năm một lần.

Nếu bỏ qua bước này và đơn xin giấy phép kinh doanh thành phố dùng tên thương hiệu, đơn sẽ bị từ chối. Làm bước này trước — mất 15 phút và mở khóa mọi thứ phía sau.

4. Hairstylist trade certification (hair salons only) 4. Chứng chỉ hành nghề thợ tóc (chỉ tiệm tóc)

Here is the single biggest distinction in Alberta salon law: hairstylist is a compulsory certified trade; nail tech and esthetician are not.

In Alberta, anyone who cuts, colours, or styles hair for paying clients must hold a Journeyperson Certificate in Hairstylist issued by Alberta Apprenticeship and Industry Training, or be a registered apprentice working under the supervision of a certified journeyperson. This is not optional. A salon that lets an uncertified person cut hair is exposed to fines and enforcement action.

There are two paths to certification:

  1. Apprenticeship path. Typically 1 to 2 years. Each 12-month period includes at least 1,450 hours of on-the-job training plus roughly 10 weeks of classroom instruction at an accredited school like M.C. College or NIWE Academy in Calgary.
  2. Trade Qualifier path. For experienced stylists without a certificate. Requires 36 months and 4,350 hours of verifiable hairstylist work experience, plus passing both a theory exam and a practical exam.

What about nails, lashes, and esthetics? None of these are compulsory trades in Alberta. You do not need a provincial certificate to work on nails, perform facials, apply lash extensions, or wax clients. Your salon still needs a municipal business licence and AHS notification, and workers still need to follow the Personal Services Standards — but there is no government-issued personal certification for these roles. This is a real point of confusion for people moving from American states where nail technicians must hold a state licence. In Alberta, you do not.

What about barbers? Barber and hairstylist are closely linked in Alberta — the skills overlap significantly, and the certification framework is similar. Barbershop owners should expect the same compulsory trade requirements for anyone cutting hair.

For current requirements, visit Tradesecrets — Hairstylist profile or call Alberta Apprenticeship directly.

Đây là sự khác biệt lớn nhất trong luật salon Alberta: thợ tóc là nghề được chứng nhận bắt buộc; thợ nail và thẩm mỹ viên thì không.

Ở Alberta, bất cứ ai cắt, nhuộm, hoặc tạo kiểu tóc cho khách trả tiền phải có Chứng chỉ Hành nghề Thợ tóc do Alberta Apprenticeship and Industry Training cấp, hoặc là thợ học việc đã đăng ký.

Có hai con đường:

  1. Con đường học nghề. Thường 1-2 năm, mỗi năm có ít nhất 1,450 giờ làm việc thực tế cộng 10 tuần học lý thuyết tại trường như M.C. College hoặc NIWE Academy ở Calgary.
  2. Con đường Trade Qualifier. Dành cho thợ có kinh nghiệm. Yêu cầu 36 tháng và 4,350 giờ kinh nghiệm, cộng với đậu kỳ thi lý thuyết và thực hành.

Còn nail, mi, và thẩm mỹ? Không có nghề nào trong số này là nghề bắt buộc ở Alberta. Bạn không cần chứng chỉ cấp tỉnh để làm nail, làm da mặt, nối mi, hoặc tẩy lông. Đây là điểm hay gây nhầm lẫn cho người đến từ các bang Mỹ nơi thợ nail phải có giấy phép bang. Ở Alberta, bạn không cần.

5. GST number and WCB account 5. Số GST và tài khoản WCB

GST registration (CRA)

Once your salon's taxable revenue exceeds $30,000 in any four consecutive calendar quarters, you must register for a GST number with the Canada Revenue Agency. Before you hit that threshold you can register voluntarily — and many salon owners do, because it lets you claim input tax credits on the GST you pay on supplies, equipment, and rent. GST registration is free and can be done online in about 20 minutes.

WCB Alberta account

Anyone with employees in Alberta must have a Workers' Compensation Board account. Sole proprietors and partners are not automatically covered — you can opt in for personal coverage if you want. Registration is free; premiums are calculated annually as a percentage of your payroll based on your industry's injury risk rating. Salons are generally in a low-risk category, so expect low premium rates per $100 of payroll.

Independent contractors working as booth renters are a grey area. If you rent a chair to someone who sets their own hours and keeps their own tips, they may not be your employee — but document this carefully with a written agreement, and when in doubt, call WCB directly to confirm status.

Đăng ký GST (CRA)

Khi doanh thu chịu thuế của salon vượt $30,000 trong bất kỳ bốn quý liên tiếp, bạn phải đăng ký số GST với Canada Revenue Agency. Đăng ký miễn phí và có thể làm trực tuyến trong khoảng 20 phút.

Tài khoản WCB Alberta

Bất kỳ ai có nhân viên ở Alberta phải có tài khoản Workers' Compensation Board. Đăng ký miễn phí; phí bảo hiểm tính hàng năm theo phần trăm tổng lương dựa trên xếp hạng rủi ro của ngành.

6. Development, occupancy, and signage permits 6. Giấy phép phát triển, công năng và biển hiệu

These are the city-level permits that most salon owners forget until the landlord or inspector asks about them. They do not apply to every salon — but when they apply, they are the biggest and slowest part of the process.

Development permit

Required if you are changing the use of a commercial space (for example, converting a retail store into a salon), adding or moving walls, or building out a completely new space. In Calgary this is handled through Planning & Development Services. In Edmonton, through the Development Permit process at Development Services. Fees vary widely: from a few hundred dollars for minor work to several thousand for a full build-out. Timeline: 4 to 8 weeks is typical.

Building & occupancy permits

If construction involves structural work, plumbing, or electrical changes, you will also need building permits and a final occupancy permit before opening. The fire inspection tied to your business licence is a separate sign-off and does not replace occupancy review.

Sign permit

Both Calgary and Edmonton require a separate sign permit for most permanent exterior signs — not covered by your business licence. Fees depend on sign size, type (wall-mounted, freestanding, projecting), and whether it's illuminated. Apply through the city's sign permit process before installing any sign visible from the street.

Tip: if you are taking over an existing salon space that was previously licensed as a salon, you can usually skip the development permit entirely — the "use" has not changed. This is one of the fastest ways to open: find a former salon location and move in.

Đây là những giấy phép cấp thành phố mà hầu hết chủ salon quên cho đến khi chủ nhà hoặc thanh tra hỏi.

Giấy phép phát triển (Development permit)

Bắt buộc nếu bạn thay đổi công năng không gian thương mại, thêm/di chuyển tường, hoặc xây mới. Phí thay đổi nhiều. Thời gian: 4-8 tuần là điển hình.

Giấy phép biển hiệu

Calgary và Edmonton đều yêu cầu giấy phép biển hiệu riêng cho hầu hết biển hiệu cố định bên ngoài — không nằm trong giấy phép kinh doanh của bạn.

Timeline: when to start each step Thời gian: bắt đầu từng bước khi nào

Here is a realistic 6-week timeline to go from nothing to legally open, assuming you are moving into an existing salon space (no development permit). Double it if you are doing a full build-out.

  • Week −6 (six weeks before opening): Register your trade name. Submit your AHS Personal Services Notification. If you're opening a hair salon, confirm every stylist's apprenticeship or journeyperson status.
  • Week −5: Apply for your municipal business licence (Calgary or Edmonton). Schedule the fire inspection. Contact landlord about required tenant improvements.
  • Week −4: Fire inspection happens. Apply for sign permit if you need exterior signage.
  • Week −3: Business licence typically issued. Start hiring staff; have them complete onboarding and Personal Services Standards training.
  • Week −2: Set up your point-of-sale and booking system. Register for GST if you expect to exceed the threshold in year one. Set up WCB if you have employees.
  • Week −1: Final walkthroughs, equipment install, staff rehearsal, soft launch with friends and family.
  • Week 0: Open for business. Inspectors may visit in the first 90 days.

If you are doing a full renovation, add 4 to 8 weeks for development and building permits at the very front of this timeline, and expect to start the licensing process after construction is done — the business licence application assumes a completed space.

Dưới đây là thời gian thực tế 6 tuần để đi từ không có gì đến mở cửa hợp pháp, giả sử bạn tiếp quản salon có sẵn (không cần giấy phép phát triển).

  • Tuần −6: Đăng ký tên thương hiệu. Nộp thông báo AHS. Xác nhận tình trạng học nghề của thợ tóc.
  • Tuần −5: Nộp đơn xin giấy phép kinh doanh thành phố. Lên lịch thanh tra cứu hỏa.
  • Tuần −4: Thanh tra cứu hỏa. Nộp đơn xin giấy phép biển hiệu nếu cần.
  • Tuần −3: Giấy phép kinh doanh được cấp. Bắt đầu tuyển dụng.
  • Tuần −2: Thiết lập POS và hệ thống đặt lịch. Đăng ký GST và WCB nếu cần.
  • Tuần −1: Kiểm tra cuối, cài đặt thiết bị, diễn tập nhân viên.
  • Tuần 0: Mở cửa kinh doanh.

Gotchas most salon owners miss Những điều chủ salon hay quên

  1. The sign permit is separate. Your business licence does not cover your exterior sign. If you install a big illuminated sign without a permit and a neighbour complains, the city will make you take it down.
  2. AHS notification is free — but mandatory. Because there's no fee, people assume it's optional. It's not. Operating without notifying AHS is a violation of the Public Health Act.
  3. Fire inspection is scheduled, not on-demand. In Calgary, fire inspection is part of the business licence application — you cannot skip it, and it adds 5+ business days to your timeline.
  4. Home-based or mobile services need a non-resident licence if you work across city lines. Calgary's non-resident fee is $785. Edmonton's is $479.
  5. Your "nail tech" cannot legally cut hair for paying clients. Even a quick bang trim or men's cut falls under the compulsory hairstylist trade. If you don't have a certified stylist, don't offer hair services at all.
  6. Booth renters are not automatically covered by your WCB. If you have booth rental agreements, each renter may need their own licence and WCB coverage. Get this in writing.
  7. Your cosmetic products must comply with federal Health Canada regulations. This includes full ingredient labelling. Cheap imports without English labels are a common AHS inspection finding.
  8. Insurance is not a licence — but your landlord will require it. Most commercial landlords require $1M–$2M in general liability insurance before they'll hand over keys. Budget $500–$1,500/year for a salon-appropriate policy.
  1. Giấy phép biển hiệu là riêng. Giấy phép kinh doanh không bao gồm biển hiệu bên ngoài.
  2. Thông báo AHS miễn phí nhưng bắt buộc. Vì không tính phí, nhiều người tưởng là tùy chọn. Không phải.
  3. Thanh tra cứu hỏa theo lịch. Ở Calgary, thanh tra cứu hỏa là một phần của đơn xin giấy phép kinh doanh — thêm 5+ ngày làm việc vào lịch trình.
  4. Dịch vụ tại nhà hoặc lưu động cần giấy phép không cư trú. Calgary: $785. Edmonton: $479.
  5. Thợ nail không được cắt tóc hợp pháp. Ngay cả cắt mái cho khách trả tiền cũng thuộc nghề thợ tóc bắt buộc.
  6. Thợ thuê ghế không tự động được bảo hiểm WCB của bạn.
  7. Sản phẩm mỹ phẩm phải tuân thủ quy định Health Canada.
  8. Bảo hiểm không phải giấy phép — nhưng chủ nhà sẽ yêu cầu. Ngân sách $500-$1,500/năm.

For Vietnamese nail salon owners Dành cho chủ tiệm nail người Việt

Alberta is one of the friendliest provinces in Canada for nail salon operators. Unlike California, Texas, or Florida — where nail technicians need an individual state licence — Alberta does not regulate nail tech or esthetician practice at the provincial level. You still need the business licence and AHS notification above, but there is no personal certificate required to do nails. If you are moving from the US, this is a significant reduction in paperwork compared to what you are used to. Alberta là một trong những tỉnh thân thiện nhất ở Canada cho chủ tiệm nail. Bạn vẫn cần giấy phép kinh doanh và thông báo AHS ở trên, nhưng không cần chứng chỉ cá nhân để làm nail. Nếu bạn chuyển từ Mỹ sang, đây là giảm đáng kể giấy tờ so với những gì bạn quen.
